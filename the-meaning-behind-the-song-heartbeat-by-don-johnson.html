The Meaning Behind The Song: Heartbeat by Don Johnson

May 2024 · 3 minute read

When “Heartbeat” by Don Johnson was released in 1986, it quickly climbed the charts and made a lasting impact on listeners. The song’s upbeat tempo and catchy melody made it a hit, but it’s the lyrics that gave it lasting power. Let’s delve into the meaning behind the song and why it resonates with so many people.

A song about love, both the highs and lows

At its core, “Heartbeat” is a love song. The lyrics describe the euphoria of falling in love, but also the heartbreak that can come with it. Lines like “When love takes over, you’re never alone” and “My heart beats like a drum, a guitar string to the strum” capture the emotion and excitement of a new relationship. Don Johnson’s raspy voice adds to the passionate tone of the song.

But “Heartbeat” doesn’t shy away from the hardships of love. Johnson sings about “times when I thought I couldn’t last” and feeling “lost and so in doubt.” These vulnerable lyrics remind listeners that even in a loving relationship, there can be struggles and doubts.

The power of music to bring people together

Beyond the lyrics, “Heartbeat” has a power that’s hard to define. It’s the kind of song that can bring people together – whether that’s on a crowded dance floor or in the privacy of their own headphones. The upbeat tempo and infectious melody make it impossible to listen without tapping your toes or bobbing your head.

And it’s not just fans who connect with “Heartbeat.” The song has been covered by numerous artists over the years, including Gloria Estefan and Kelly Clarkson. In a 2014 interview with The Huffington Post, Clarkson shared why she chose to cover the song:

“‘Heartbeat’ is such a great song. You can instantly see it in your head, like the music video and him on the boat – it’s just a great visual. But also the message behind it is happy and I feel like sometimes you don’t get that a lot in love songs. There are always those breakup love songs or ‘oh, life is hard’ love songs, but it’s very rare to find a love song that celebrates being happy and in love.”

The personal significance of “Heartbeat”

For many people, music is tied to personal memories and emotions. “Heartbeat” is no exception. Its popularity during the 80s means it became the soundtrack of many people’s youth, and the song continues to resonate with listeners today.

When I asked my friend Susan why “Heartbeat” is special to her, she shared that it was her first slow dance with her high school sweetheart. “It’s that song that immediately brings me back to those early days of love and innocence,” she said.

For others, “Heartbeat” is a reminder of lost love. Maybe it was a relationship that didn’t work out, or a loved one who passed away. But even in those moments of sadness, the song can provide comfort and a sense of connection.

The legacy of “Heartbeat”

It’s been over three decades since Don Johnson released “Heartbeat,” and yet the song remains beloved by fans young and old. Its message of love and connection is timeless, and its infectious melody continues to bring people together.

In a world where we often feel divided, music has the power to unite us. “Heartbeat” is a perfect example of this. Whether you’re dancing with a loved one or belting out the chorus in your car, this classic song will always have a place in our hearts.
