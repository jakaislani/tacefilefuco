Who Was Rodney Pitts? Firefighter Killed After Rowhomes Catch Fire In Northwest Baltimore

April 2024 · 3 minute read

Fire officials said on Friday that 31-year-old Baltimore resident Rodney Pitts III, unfortunately, passed away from his wounds after a heroic attempt with other firemen who heroically entered a burning rowhouse at 3:45 p.m. in the 5200 block of Linden Heights Avenue.

Four firefighters were hospitalized on Thursday as a result of the incident’s serious burn injuries, although two of them have already been released.

Who is Rodney Pitt?

Rodney Pitts, a devoted firefighter and EMT who had only recently joined the department, had been serving actively since August. He showed his devotion to his hometown of Baltimore by working at Engine 29 in Park Heights.

Pitts, a native of Baltimore, had a great desire to support his community, and his family expressed this desire during their meeting with Mayor Brandon Scott on Thursday night.

It is clear that Rodney made a significant sacrifice for his town out of his unwavering love for our city, gladly risking his life and sadly losing it.

Rodney Pitts case explained into details

The powerful fire consumed four rowhouses on Linden Heights Avenue on Thursday. According to city housing records, one of these houses was rented, two were unoccupied properties, and one was the owner’s residence. When the firefighters were confronted with the overpowering and fierce intensity of the blaze, Pitts found himself within one of the houses that was still occupied, located between two vacant buildings.

During this difficult scenario, three of the injured firefighters were actively engaged in rescue operations.

Pitts passed away less than two years after a tragic incident in which three firefighters perished and a fourth suffered serious injuries while battling a vacant rowhouse fire on South Stricker Street in the Central Southwest Baltimore neighborhood of New Southwest/Mount Clare.

This specific fire stands out as one of the most catastrophic and fatal disasters the city’s firefighting community has ever experienced.

One suspect was detained following the incident, and the fire investigation and homicide divisions of the Baltimore Police are conducting investigations.
