Parents & Family Background :

Valerie, who was born on April 25, 1958, is originally from Rochester, Minnesota.
Her parents were Dr. Eugene Q. Parr, who was late, and Joan Lykins Parr, who was late. Her father was a specialist in orthopaedic surgery (passed away in 2019). At the time of the birth of Valerie, Dr. Eugene was finishing his internship at the Mayo Clinic.


Valerie’s late mother was a trained teacher who taught until she passed away at the Immanuel Baptist Church Sunday School as well.
Valerie, the youngest of her siblings, has two siblings, Eugene Parr Jr. and Jeffrey W. Parr. Eugene is Minister of the Baptist Campus for Morehead State University.

Married Life :

For over three decades, Valerie is married to her husband, William Hill, who is originally from Long Island. The couple met they were both students at Wheaton College. They have been married since September 1985.


Valerie has two sons with William Hill: Gene Hill (born in 1993) and Jeffrey Hill (born in 1995). Gene was just six months old when Valerie debuted in 1993 on QVC! The boys are named after Valerie’s brothers.
Gene is a history teacher, and Jeffrey studied economics.
Valerie raised her family in Montville, New Jersey, where she continues to reside with her husband.

Early Life & Education :

Her family relocated to Lexington, Kentucky, when Valerie was three years old, where she was growing up. She attended primary school in Glendover, followed by high school in Tates Creek, where she graduated from in 1975.


In 1974, she was the recipient of the Junior Miss Fayette County Pageant and won the state beauty competition as well. At the state stage, she also competed and was the second runner-up. Her skill was playing a piece of piano from Rhapsody in Blue, showing her musical ability.
Valerie participated in the student government, pep band, and chorus and was very involved in extra-curricular sports in high school.
Later on, in Wilmore, Kentucky, Valerie attended Asbury College from 1975 until 197She initially pursued a major in piano performance, but subsequently earned a degree in speech and secondary education.
She then earned a Master’s degree from Wheaton College in Illinois, followed by a stay at the Kellogg School of Business at Northwestern University in Evanston, Illinois, studying non-profit marketing.

Valerie Parr Hill Net Worth, Collection & TV Career :

In the 1980s, after college, Valerie started out as a public official, at the same time fundraising for higher education, wildlife safety, and fine arts schools.
She was enthusiastic about hospitality and started making festive home ornaments to be marketed as a consequence of her interest.
In 1991, Parr founded her own business named The Valerie Parr Group.
As she made touch with QVC while she was representing wreath vendors, a career turning point for Valerie arrived. QVC got involved in the wreaths and offered her screen time to show them.
In September 1993, she had her first appearance on QVC, in the Decorating with Wreaths show. The program was presented by Jane Treacy afterward. In 1996, she entered into an exclusive relationship with QVC and continues to be one of the most popular hosts for home decor.
She wrote her own book on home decor in October 2000, named Decorating for the Holidays.
In other QVC series, such as Heartfelt Houses, Heartfelt Holidays and Home Fragrances, Parr Hill has also been used. Her take on interior design is to build a home space that is warm and inviting, which makes her decor really common for family homes and festive seasons.
